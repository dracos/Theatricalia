Help & Frequently Asked Questions

As this site has only just launched, we haven’t got many frequently asked questions yet. If you’re puzzled by something on the site, enjoy trying to work out the solution. If you’ve found a mistake on the site, please do not get in touch – create an account and correct the mistake yourself!

My local theatre is missing; I’m missing; This famous actor/ play/ theatre/ production is missing!
Not a question, but easy to point in the right direction. Theatricalia is based around productions – if a play has not been and is not going to be performed, or if a theatre has never or is not going to host a production, it does not yet need to be on the site. To add a production, follow our simple interface (you can also follow links from any existing place or play page), and you can add any missing play, place, or people along the way.
I think your design/ UI is rubbish.
Still not a question. Good for you; everyone’s entitled to their opinion.
That’s not a Google Map, or even a normal OpenStreetMap map.
That’s correct (and not a question). It’s made using CloudMade’s map style editor, and is an altered version of the RideTheCity 3 style.
How does the search work? I typed Katie Stevens and it knew I meant Katy Stephens.
At last! It’s magic, in the Arthur C. Clarke sense. homophone matching and Jarow distance matching.
The space between the “C” and the “Clarke” in the previous question is smaller than a normal space. You’ve gone a bit overboard typographically, haven’t you?
No. Websites can try and follow The Elements of Typographic Style; it’s just nicer. I am happy every time I see the profile pages using the possessive apostrophe correctly; that dates are all printed prettily; that the only ' and " on this site will be if a play title includes feet and inches (and at that point I’ll have to write some code for that). Please make sure your contributions use correct spelling and grammar.
